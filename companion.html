Rabbit Care 101

Care Diet Habitat Companionship

Rabbits are social animals that thrive on interaction with both their own kind and humans. Introducing a compatible rabbit companion can provide enrichment, mental stimulation, and emotional support for your rabbit. When introducing rabbits, it's essential to follow proper introductions to ensure they get along and prevent aggression. Supervise their interactions initially and provide separate spaces for each rabbit to retreat if needed. Human interaction is also crucial for rabbits' social development and well-being. Spending time with your rabbit, offering gentle petting, and engaging in interactive play sessions strengthens the bond between you and your rabbit. Talk to your rabbit, offer treats, and provide opportunities for them to explore their surroundings under your supervision. Regular handling and positive interactions with humans help build trust and confidence in your rabbit, contributing to their overall happiness and quality of life. By fostering companionship with both other rabbits and humans, you can create a supportive and enriching environment for your rabbit to thrive emotionally and socially. With proper care, attention, and companionship, your rabbit will not only enjoy a happy and healthy life but also form meaningful connections with both their own kind and their human caregivers.
